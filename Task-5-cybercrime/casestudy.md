# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email or message that looks like it is from a bank or a well-known company. The message often contains a link and urges the user to take immediate action, such as updating account details. When the user clicks the link, they are taken to a fake website that looks real. Once they enter their information, it is stolen by the attacker.

Students and working professionals are common targets because they frequently use online services. The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

Phishing attacks are becoming more advanced, making it important for users to stay alert and verify the authenticity of any online communication before taking action.
